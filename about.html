About FreeCPTCodeFinder.com

FreeCPTCodeFinder.com is an educational CPT coding and billing resource built to help residents, students, APPs, RNFAs, coders, and clinicians quickly find codes for case logs and documentation workflows while learning practical billing concepts that matter in real clinical practice.

Founder and developer

Graydon Stallard, DO, FACOS, FACS is Board Certified in both General Surgery and Surgical Critical Care, with clinical expertise in general surgery, trauma, and acute care surgery. He serves as the Director of Medical Education at CaroMont Regional Medical Center and is an Assistant Professor of Surgery at VCOM, Edward Via College of Osteopathic Medicine in Spartanburg, South Carolina.

In his educational and clinical roles, Dr. Stallard has worked closely with physician assistant students, RNFA students, nurse practitioner students, medical students, residents, and other learners as they participate in surgical cases and develop their clinical documentation skills. Through that experience, he recognized a common challenge: learners often struggle to find the correct CPT codes for cases they have scrubbed or participated in, especially when trying to complete required case logs.

Dr. Stallard created FreeCPTCodeFinder.com to help address that gap. The site was designed as an educational CPT code lookup and coding resource for students, residents, APPs, RNFAs, coders, and clinicians who need a practical way to find CPT codes while also learning the basics of surgical billing and documentation.

How content is created

Blog articles and educational guides are written by Dr. Stallard with the assistance of AI tools, then edited before publication for clarity, educational value, and alignment with the site’s purpose.

What this site is and is not

FreeCPTCodeFinder.com is intended for educational coding support only. It does not provide medical advice, legal advice, compliance advice, or a guarantee of payer reimbursement. Users should always verify coding decisions against current CPT, CMS, NCCI, institutional, and payer-specific guidance.
